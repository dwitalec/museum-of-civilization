historicalContext: "The original Apple Watch, released in April 2015, was the first mass-market wearable to successfully merge health monitoring, smartphone notifications, and fashion into a single device. It was controversial at launch — reviewers debated whether it solved a real problem — but it quietly redefined the relationship between people and their devices. Where a phone requires you to reach for it, a watch taps your wrist. Where a phone is a distraction, a watch attempted to be a filter. The Apple Watch became the best-selling watch in the world by 2017, overtaking Rolex, and established wearables as a permanent category."

personalStory: "When I bought my first Apple Watch, I was just trying to stay cool with the latest Apple tech. Having been a bit slow to the draw on the iPhone, I had to get the Apple Watch right away. It was… fine, to start. It told time well enough, though charging every night seemed a bit annoying at first. Where it really took off for me, and why I still wear it every day, was in its health tracking – and specifically heart rate monitoring for me while I worked out. That enabled me to have a good feel for where I stood relative to my target heart rate, and I like to think made my workouts a bit more efficient."
