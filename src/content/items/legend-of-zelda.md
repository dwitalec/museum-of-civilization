historicalContext: "Released in Japan in 1986 and North America in 1987, The Legend of Zelda for the NES was a foundational moment in game design. It was among the first console games to feature a persistent save system — enabled by the gold cartridge's internal battery — which meant for the first time, a game could have a world large enough to require more than one sitting to finish. It introduced open-world exploration, puzzle-gating, and the concept of a game as an adventure rather than a score challenge. Its influence on game design is still visible in virtually every action-adventure game made today."

personalStory: "I was absolutely blown away when I got my 8-bit Nintendo in the late 1980’s as a young teen. Super Mario Brothers and other basic games were fun and far above the Atari games I had played before, but The Legend of Zelda took it to another level. There was a huge open world to explore, and your progress was saved with an internal battery! I remember mapping out Hyrule to figure out where all of the dungeons were and where various secrets remained. My friend Josh Cocanour and I would spend hours with it. And its beautiful gold cartridge just made it feel special."
