historicalContext: "Introduced by Motorola in January 1996, the StarTAC was the world's first clamshell mobile phone and one of the smallest and lightest ever made at the time. Weighing just 88 grams, it was a radical departure from the brick phones of the early cellular era and became an instant cultural icon — appearing in films, on television, and in the hands of anyone who wanted to signal that they were living in the future. The StarTAC established that mobile phones could be fashionable objects, not just functional tools, and set expectations for miniaturization and design that drove the entire industry forward through the late 1990s and 2000s."

personalStory: "I believe the Motorola StarTAC flip phone, on the Sprint network, was my 2nd cell phone, after a large blocky one I originally had. Relative to that one (and most phones prior to the StarTac), the StarTac was so small and stealth. It fit in your pocket and allowed for ultimate mobility. Cell phones would continue to get smaller and smaller, until the iPhone changed that dynamic and they started growing in size."
