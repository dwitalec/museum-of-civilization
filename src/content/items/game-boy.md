historicalContext: "Released by Nintendo in 1989, the original Game Boy was not the most powerful handheld of its era — it had a blurry green-gray screen and no backlight — but it outsold every competitor by an enormous margin. Its secret was the battery life and the library: Tetris, bundled at launch, turned it into a cultural phenomenon. The Game Boy established that gaming was not a living room activity but a personal one. It sold over 118 million units across its lifetime and seeded the handheld market that would eventually produce the Nintendo DS, the PSP, and the smartphone game industry."

personalStory: "Tetris and the GameBoy go hand-in-hand for me. Despite its key attribute of portability, I remember playing it most in my room growing up. The other games – like the Zelda game shown in the picture – were nice, but those were really best for the main Nintendo system. Tetris and its blocks fit perfectly in the small GameBoy screen. I logged so many hours trying to master it."
