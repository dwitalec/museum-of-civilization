historicalContext: "When Steve Jobs introduced the first iPod in 2001, he promised 1,000 songs in your pocket. When the iPod Nano launched in 2005, he pulled it out of the small pocket in his jeans. Thinner than a pencil, replacing the Mini's mechanical hard drive with flash memory, the Nano was a design statement as much as a product. It changed what consumers expected from electronics: that function and beauty were not a tradeoff. The Nano also represented the peak of Apple's pre-iPhone identity and the cultural moment when digital music finished killing the CD."

personalStory: "I wasn’t an early adopter of the iPod, but I did love my iPod Nano, which came out in 2005. For me the Nano was great to use while out on a run, or just around the house. With it, I shifted away from CDs, spending many an afternoon porting my CDs onto iTunes to enable their play on my iPod. Of course, as smartphones became the norm, they replaced the iPod. But we still used our iPod Nano to play nighttime songs for Claire for many years."
