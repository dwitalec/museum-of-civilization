historicalContext: "Tesla had already proved that an electric car could be desirable with the Roadster and aspirational with the Model S. The Model 3, unveiled in March 2016 and first delivered in July 2017, was the bet that it could be mass-market. Nearly 400,000 people put down $1,000 deposits before a single production car existed. What arrived was a genuinely driver-focused sedan — minimalist interior, a single 15-inch touchscreen replacing nearly every physical control, and a drivetrain with no transmission, no exhaust, and no engine noise. The range anxiety that had shadowed electric vehicles for decades began to recede as the Supercharger network expanded. The Model 3 forced every major automaker to accelerate their own EV timelines. By 2023 it had become the best-selling car in the world — not just the best-selling electric car, but all cars. It marked the moment the internal combustion engine stopped being the obvious default."

personalStory: "I’ve never been a car guy, per se, which is probably why the Tesla Model 3 appealed so much to me. It was a tech toy more than a traditional car, which I loved. It also did absolutely everything differently, from the controls being mostly on a touchscreen, to the regenerative braking (which meant you rarely had to use traditional brakes), to the keyless entry (just using your phone). I remember the first night I got home and freaking out because I didn’t know how to turn it off. (It turns out it just turns off itself after you put it in park.) 
I bought the Self-Driving package with the car when I bought it in 2019, and loved playing with all of the autonomous driving stuff from the start, even if most of that technology was ahead of its time. The last time I saw my Dad healthy, at my brother’s 50-year old birthday party in October 2019, I remember showing him how I could remotely control the car to drive to me in the parking lot. It was a parlor trick, but a fun one nonetheless. I also took solace in knowing that my dad likely appreciated that I finally bought an American car, even if he didn’t really get the appeal of an electric car."
