historicalContext: "When Apple unveiled the iPhone 7 in September 2016, the headline wasn't the camera or the chip — it was what was missing: the headphone jack. The AirPods, announced the same day, were Apple's answer to the outrage. Few took them seriously at first. The little white stems dangling from people's ears became a meme before they became a status symbol. But within a year the meme flipped: AirPods were everywhere, and not owning a pair felt like the odd choice. They popularized the true wireless earbud category almost single-handedly, kickstarted a wave of competing products from every major electronics brand, and redefined what people expected from audio. The W1 chip's seamless pairing, the magnetic charging case, the tap controls — each a small detail that together made wireless feel genuinely ready. Apple sold over 100 million pairs in 2021 alone."

personalStory: "AirPods were the classic Apple innovation, in my mind. We already had Bluetooth, and we had some wireless headphones already. And wired headphones seemed fine, so did we really need to go wireless, especially if you had to connect and reconnect them to Bluetooth endlessly? But Apple made it all seamless and easy. I remember thinking that they’d come out of my ears all the time, but without a wire pulling at them, this rarely happened. And it was just so easy to automatically connect to your iPhone. It made listening so simple. For me, it launched my podcast era – always listening to something on my AirPods when I did the dishes or other chores around the house."
